# Remittances — Source Notes

## Source Quality Assessment

### Tier 1: Official/Institutional (High Confidence)
- **World Bank KNOMAD** — The global gold standard for remittance value data. Compiles balance-of-payments data from central banks worldwide. Published twice yearly (April and October Migration and Development Briefs). Value figures ($905B global, $685B to LMICs) are highly reliable. Does NOT track transaction counts.
- **World Bank Remittance Prices Worldwide (RPW)** — Quarterly survey of remittance costs across 365+ corridors. The 6.35% average cost figure is authoritative and widely cited.
- **Federal Reserve FEDS Notes** — Academic-quality research on remittance cyclicality. Useful for macro context but not for transaction-level data.

### Tier 2: Company Filings (Medium-High Confidence)
- **Western Union quarterly/annual results** — Publicly traded (NYSE: WU). Reports transaction counts and revenue by segment. The Q4 2024 figure of 75M transactions is from an audited filing. Note: WU revenue has been declining even as volumes are flat, reflecting price pressure from digital competitors.
- **Wise annual report** — Publicly traded (LSE: WISE). Reports volume ($145B) and customer count but not transaction count directly. Transaction count estimated from volume / avg txn.
- **Block/PayPal filings** — Useful for understanding digital P2P overlap but don't break out cross-border personal transfers.

### Tier 3: Industry Reports (Medium Confidence)
- **FXC Intelligence** — Specialist cross-border payments research firm. Publishes remittance market analysis and company-specific data. Paywall for full reports; free summaries used.
- **William Blair** — Investment bank with a payments research team. Annual remittance market report provides useful growth forecasts and company analysis. Paywall.
- **Grand View Research / Precedence Research / Business Research Insights** — Market research firms with remittance forecasts. Methodologies vary; projections used for cross-referencing our own models, not as primary sources.

### Tier 4: Estimates (Low Confidence)
- **MoneyGram** — Taken private by Madison Dearborn Partners in August 2023. Post-privatization data is very limited. Pre-2023 figures (~50M customers, ~$200B annual volume) are from the last public filings.
- **Remitly, WorldRemit, Xoom** — Limited public disclosure. Volume estimates from press releases and industry analyses.
- **Bank channel** — No aggregated data exists for bank-to-bank personal remittance transactions. Our $300B / 400M txn estimate is very rough.
- **Transaction count overall** — The 1.8B figure is our derivation. We have not found any published source that provides a global remittance transaction count. This is a genuine data gap in the literature.

## Data Gaps

1. **No global transaction count** — This is the single biggest gap. The World Bank, BIS, CPMI, and FATF all track values but not counts.
2. **Informal/hawala volume** — By definition unmeasured. The +50% adjustment is itself an estimate.
3. **Bank channel granularity** — Banks don't separately report "personal remittance" vs. "business payment" in their cross-border transfer statistics.
4. **Corridor-level transaction data** — Value data exists for major corridors (US-Mexico, Gulf-India, etc.) but transaction counts do not.
5. **Post-privatization MoneyGram** — Data becomes increasingly stale after 2023.
