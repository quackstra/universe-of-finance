# Forex — Key Assumptions

## Transaction Definition
- One FX trade = one execution between two counterparties
- Includes spot, forwards, swaps, and options
- Does NOT include FX futures (covered in ETD category)
- Does NOT include retail CFD trades (these are synthetic, not actual FX)
- FX swaps count as ONE transaction (not two legs)

## Value Data
- BIS triennial survey is conducted in April every 3 years
- "Net-net" basis adjusts for double-counting between reporting dealers
- 2024 turnover interpolated linearly between 2022 ($7.5T) and 2025 ($9.6T) surveys

## Trade Count Estimation
- CLS settles ~20% of global FX by trade count (500K/day)
- Average institutional ticket size: $3-5 million
- Retail FX CFDs excluded from count (synthetic exposure, internally netted)
- Central estimate: ~3 million trades/day

## Market Hours
- FX markets operate ~23.5 hours/day, 5 days/week
- ~252 trading days per year
- TPS calculated over 24h (conservative) since FX is truly global

## Double-Counting Notes
- FX derivatives (swaps, forwards, options) overlap with the OTC Derivatives category
- Per taxonomy rules: counted here as FX, noted as overlap
- FX swaps used for funding purposes may overstate "trading" activity
- CLS settlement involves both legs of each trade but counts as one trade

## Projection Assumptions
- Baseline CAGR (6%) based on 15-year historical trend
- High growth (9%) assumes retail FX expansion + crypto-fiat flows
- Conservative (3%) assumes regulatory tightening and consolidation
- Trade count assumed to grow proportionally with value turnover
