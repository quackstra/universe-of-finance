# Tax & Government Payments — Source Notes

> Extended notes on each source consulted during research. Accessed 2026-03-26.

---

## Primary Sources

### 1. OECD — Revenue Statistics 2025
- **URL:** https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/2025/12/revenue-statistics-2025_07ca0a8e.html
- **Key data:** OECD average tax-to-GDP ratio reached record 34.1% in 2024; 33/36 countries saw revenue increases; range from 18.3% (Mexico) to 45.2% (Denmark)
- **Reliability:** :green_circle: High — OECD is the gold standard for cross-country tax statistics
- **Notes:** Covers 38 OECD member countries. Provides final data through 2023 and provisional 2024 estimates. Does not include transaction counts — only aggregate revenue figures.

### 2. OECD — Tax Revenue Trends 1965-2024
- **URL:** https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/2025/12/revenue-statistics-2025_07ca0a8e/full-report/tax-revenue-trends-1965-2024_98c75833.html
- **Key data:** 60-year time series of tax-to-GDP ratios; labour taxes were the primary driver of 2024's record revenues
- **Reliability:** :green_circle: High
- **Notes:** Excellent for historic trend analysis. Tax structures have shifted over time — income taxes declining as share, VAT/GST increasing.

### 3. IMF — Fiscal Monitor (April 2024)
- **URL:** https://www.imf.org/-/media/files/publications/fiscal-monitor/2024/april/english/ch1.pdf
- **Key data:** Global fiscal policy analysis; government debt trajectories; social benefit spending analysis
- **Reliability:** :green_circle: High
- **Notes:** Focuses on fiscal policy and debt sustainability, not transaction volumes. Useful for understanding the macro context of government payments.

### 4. IMF — Government Expenditure DataMapper
- **URL:** https://www.imf.org/external/datamapper/exp@FPP
- **Key data:** Government expenditure as % of GDP by country, interactive tool
- **Reliability:** :green_circle: High
- **Notes:** Covers ~190 countries. General government spending averaged ~33-40% of GDP across advanced economies, ~25-35% in emerging markets.

### 5. US Social Security Administration — Annual Statistical Supplement 2024
- **URL:** https://www.ssa.gov/policy/docs/statcomps/supplement/2024/highlights.html
- **Key data:** 60.1M OASI recipients; 8.3M DI recipients; 183.9M payroll tax contributors; $1,327.2B in OASI costs
- **Reliability:** :green_circle: High — official US government data
- **Notes:** US-specific but useful as a benchmark. US Social Security is the world's largest single disbursement programme.

### 6. US IRS — Filing Season Statistics
- **URL:** https://www.irs.gov/newsroom/filing-season-statistics-by-year
- **Key data:** 161.1M individual returns filed in FY2024; 147.1M e-filed (91% e-filing rate)
- **Reliability:** :green_circle: High — official IRS data
- **Notes:** The 91% e-filing rate shows advanced digitalisation in the US. Many developing nations are far behind. US processes ~40% of total OECD individual returns.

### 7. World Bank — Government Final Consumption Expenditure
- **URL:** https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/NE.CON.GOVT.CD
- **Key data:** Time series of government consumption expenditure in current USD for all countries
- **Reliability:** :green_circle: High
- **Notes:** "Final consumption expenditure" is narrower than total government spending — excludes transfers and capital expenditure. Useful for cross-country comparison but not directly usable as "government payment value."

### 8. Our World in Data — Government Spending
- **URL:** https://ourworldindata.org/government-spending
- **Key data:** Visualisations of government spending as % of GDP from 1880 to present
- **Reliability:** :green_circle: High (uses IMF source data)
- **Notes:** Excellent for understanding the long-run trend of growing government spending.

## Secondary Sources

### 9. Tax Foundation — OECD Tax Revenue by Country
- **URL:** https://taxfoundation.org/data/all/global/oecd-tax-revenue-by-country/
- **Key data:** Comparative analysis of OECD tax structures — income tax, social insurance, VAT, corporate tax breakdowns
- **Reliability:** :yellow_circle: Medium — analytical layer on top of OECD data

### 10. CBO — 2024 Long-Term Projections for Social Security
- **URL:** https://www.cbo.gov/publication/60392
- **Key data:** US Social Security trust fund depletion projected by 2035; projections of future beneficiary counts
- **Reliability:** :green_circle: High — Congressional Budget Office

## Data Gaps

The following data would significantly improve this analysis but was not available:

1. **Global aggregate tax filing counts** — no organisation tracks total tax returns filed worldwide
2. **VAT-registered business counts by country** — scattered across national tax authorities, not aggregated
3. **Benefits disbursement transaction counts** — individual country social security agencies track this but no global aggregate exists
4. **Real-time tax reporting transaction volumes** — India's GST e-invoicing system and EU ViDA generate massive transaction counts but these are not yet fully reported publicly
5. **Customs duty payment counts** — WTO tracks trade volumes but not the number of discrete duty payment transactions
