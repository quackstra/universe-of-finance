Commodities — Source Notes

Primary Sources

FIA (Futures Industry Association)

  • Authority: Industry body; publishes annual global ETD volume review
  • Coverage: 85 exchanges in 33 countries, all asset classes including commodities
  • Metrics: Contracts traded (annual, monthly), open interest
  • Limitation: Commodity-specific breakdowns require WFE cross-reference; not always published at granular level
  • URL: https://www.fia.org/fia/articles/2024-annual-etd-volume-review

ICE (Intercontinental Exchange)

  • Authority: Major commodity exchange operator
  • Coverage: Energy (oil, gas, power), environmental (carbon), agriculture (sugar, coffee, cotton)
  • Metrics: Contract volumes, open interest, notional value
  • Key data: Record 1.2B commodity contracts in 2024; 655M oil contracts; 20.4M environmental contracts
  • URL: https://ir.theice.com/press/news-details/2025/ICE-Announces-That-a-Record-2-Billion-Contracts-Traded-in-2024/default.aspx

CME Group

  • Authority: World's largest derivatives exchange
  • Coverage: Energy (NYMEX), metals (COMEX), agriculture (CBOT), plus financial derivatives
  • Metrics: ADV by asset class, annual records
  • Limitation: Reports total exchange ADV; commodity-specific breakdown requires disaggregation
  • URL: https://www.cmegroup.com/media-room/press-releases/

LME (London Metal Exchange)

  • Authority: Primary global base metals exchange
  • Coverage: Aluminium, copper, zinc, nickel, tin, lead, cobalt, lithium
  • Metrics: Lots traded (annual, monthly, daily)
  • Key data: 179M lots in 2024, ADV 702,639
  • URL: https://www.lme.com/en/market-data/reports-and-data/volumes/annual-volumes

ESMA (European Securities and Markets Authority)

  • Authority: EU financial regulator
  • Coverage: EU ETS carbon market
  • Metrics: Trading volume (tCO2), transaction count, value
  • Key data: 13.7B tCO2 across 4.7M transactions, EUR 781B in 2024
  • URL: https://www.esma.europa.eu/

Data Gaps

  • No single source for global commodity ETD volume (requires aggregation)
  • Physical OTC commodity trading is not centrally reported
  • Chinese exchange data quality varies
  • Notional value is not standardized across commodity contracts
