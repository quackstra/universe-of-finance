Remittances — Assumptions

Key Assumptions

1. Transaction Count Methodology

  • The World Bank does NOT publish transaction counts for remittances, only values
  • Our 1.8B estimate is built bottom-up from provider-level data
  • Western Union is the most transparent (quarterly transaction figures in earnings releases)
  • For other providers, we estimate: txn count = volume / avg transaction size
  • This methodology has significant uncertainty (+/- 30%)

2. Average Transaction Size

  • Declining over time from ~$550 (2019) to ~$503 (2024)
  • Driven by digital channel mix shift: digital avg is $200-400 vs. traditional $500-600
  • This is consistent with industry reporting (Remitly avg ~$300, Western Union avg ~$450)
  • Bank wires skew the average up (~$750-1,500 per transfer)

3. Informal Flows

  • Excluded from all figures
  • World Bank estimates informal flows could be 50% of formal flows
  • If included, the true total could be ~$1.35 trillion and ~2.7 billion transactions
  • Hawala networks dominate certain corridors (Somalia, Afghanistan, parts of South Asia)
  • Crypto remittances are growing but still <2% of formal volume

4. Provider Market Share

  • Western Union: ~15% of global value, ~17% of transactions (smaller average than banks)
  • Wise: ~16% of value but serves a different demographic (expats, larger transfers)
  • Banks: ~33% of value but ~22% of transactions (higher average sizes)
  • These shares are rough estimates — no single source publishes comprehensive market share data

5. Digital Penetration

  • Estimated at ~40% of total remittance transactions in 2024 (up from ~15% in 2019)
  • MoneyGram reached 50% digital in Q1 2023; Western Union is at ~25-30% digital
  • Digital-first players (Wise, Remitly, WorldRemit) are 100% digital by definition
  • This shift is the primary driver of our assumption that transaction counts grow faster than values

6. Projection Assumptions

  • Baseline: Migration patterns continue, digital adoption accelerates gradually, costs decline slowly
  • High Growth: Digital rails (including stablecoin settlements) reduce costs to <2%, enabling more frequent smaller transfers; new corridors open
  • Conservative: Immigration restrictions in key sender countries (US, Gulf, EU) reduce migrant populations; regulatory burden increases compliance costs

7. Double-Counting Considerations

  • Cross-border PayPal/Venmo transfers could be counted in both P2P and Remittances
  • We attempt to avoid this by restricting P2P to domestic transfers
  • In practice, some leakage exists — a US-Mexico Venmo transfer is P2P or remittance?
  • Magnitude of overlap is small (<$5B)

8. Year/Period Alignment

  • World Bank data is calendar year
  • Western Union reports calendar year (Q4 results in Feb)
  • Wise reports fiscal year ending March — FY2025 data covers Apr 2024 - Mar 2025
  • Minor misalignment accepted
