# Remittance Corridors Deep Dive — Mapping the Top 10 Flows

> Part of the [Universe of Finance](../../../README.md) project.
> **Last Updated**: 2026-07-19 (Run 13)
> **Complements**: [Middle East](../middle-east/REPORT.md) (the Gulf is the origin of most
> high-volume corridors) and [Remittances](../../../payments/remittances/REPORT.md).

---

## Executive Summary

The [Remittances](../../../payments/remittances/REPORT.md) category counts ~57 TPS net
across ~1.8B transactions. That headline hides enormous structural variation: remittances
are not a uniform global flow but a set of **discrete corridors**, each with its own ticket
size, formal/informal split, and transaction cadence.

This deep dive maps the **10 highest-volume corridors**, which together carry an estimated
**~19 TPS** — roughly a third of all remittance transaction *count* — and reveals the
**informal (hawala) shadow** that the formal figure misses.

Two headline facts frame the analysis:

1. **Formal remittances to low/middle-income countries hit ~$685B in 2024**, but including
   informal flows the true figure **may exceed $1.1 trillion**
   ([World Bank](https://blogs.worldbank.org/en/peoplemove/in-2024--remittance-flows-to-low--and-middle-income-countries-ar))
   — a **~60% informal uplift**.

2. **The Gulf is the world's remittance engine room.** Saudi Arabia (~$39B) and the UAE
   (~$50B) originate corridors to India, Pakistan, and the Philippines that rival the giant
   US→Mexico flow — but with far higher informal shares.

## The Hawala Shadow

The formal-vs-informal split is the defining uncertainty. Three patterns:

- **Mature/regulated corridors (US-originated)**: informal share is low (3–8%). Decades of
  MTO competition (Western Union, Remitly, Wise) and AML enforcement pushed flows formal.
- **Gulf → South Asia**: informal share is high (15–30%). Hawala remains cost- and
  speed-competitive, and cultural trust networks are deep. Pakistan has run government
  incentive schemes specifically to shift flows *from* hawala *to* formal channels.
- **Open-border corridors (India↔Nepal)**: informal share is highest (~40%), because cash
  hand-carry across an open border is frictionless. India←Nepal may carry **$5–6B in
  unreported hawala/cash flow** on top of ~$8B formal
  ([World Bank](https://blogs.worldbank.org/en/peoplemove/in-2024--remittance-flows-to-low--and-middle-income-countries-ar)).

### Informal transaction uplift

Applying the ~60% global informal value uplift (and noting informal tickets skew smaller,
cash-based) to the top-10 corridors suggests an **additional ~5–8 TPS of uncounted
informal transactions** in these corridors alone. Globally, this is consistent with Run 12's
Shadow-Economy finding of **+22–36 TPS** from informal remittances across all corridors.

## Cross-Reference: The Gulf's Role

Of the top 10 corridors, **five originate in the Gulf** (UAE→India, Saudi→India,
Saudi→Pakistan, UAE→Pakistan, UAE→Philippines, plus the Kuwait/Qatar group). Combined Gulf
corridor volume in this table is **~$74B → ~7.5 TPS** — matching the outbound remittance
figure derived independently in the [Middle East deep dive](../middle-east/REPORT.md)
(~7.5 TPS regional remittance TPS). **The two analyses triangulate.** ✅

This is the value/count signature again: the Gulf's ~7.5 TPS of remittance *transactions*
carries ~$74B/yr — high value per transaction, originated by ~28M migrant workers.

## Key Findings

1. **Remittances are corridors, not a blob.** The top 10 corridors carry a third of global
   remittance count but span a 4.7× range in ticket size ($150 Nepal → $700 US→India) and a
   13× range in informal share (3% US→Mexico → 40% India←Nepal).

2. **The Gulf and the US are the two engine rooms.** US-originated corridors are large and
   formal; Gulf-originated corridors are large and *informal-heavy*. Any global remittance
   count is dominated by these two origins.

3. **Hawala is the measurement frontier.** The ~60% informal uplift ($685B → $1.1T) means
   nearly as much remittance value moves *outside* formal rails as inside. By transaction
   count this is ~+5–8 TPS in the top corridors, ~+22–36 TPS globally (per Run 12).

4. **Independent triangulation holds.** Gulf corridor TPS derived here (~7.5) matches the
   Middle East deep dive's outbound remittance TPS (~7.5) — a cross-check that raises
   confidence in both. ✅

5. **Ticket size is the hidden lever.** Because TPS = value ÷ ticket ÷ time, corridors with
   small tickets (Nepal $150) generate disproportionate transaction *count* relative to
   their value — the opposite of the SWF/oil pattern.
