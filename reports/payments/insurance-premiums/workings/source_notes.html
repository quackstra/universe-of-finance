Insurance Premium Payments — Source Notes

Extended notes on each source consulted during research. Accessed 2026-03-28.

Primary Sources

1. Swiss Re — sigma No. 3/2025: World Insurance Premium Overview

  • URL: https://www.swissre.com/institute/research/sigma-research/sigma-2025-03.html
  • Key data: ~$7.2T global gross written premiums in 2024; life ~$3.0T, non-life ~$4.2T; +5.9% YoY
  • Reliability: 🟢 High — Swiss Re sigma is the gold standard for global insurance market data, published annually for 50+ years
  • Notes: Covers 147 markets. Methodology is well-documented. Figures are in nominal USD. Forex effects can distort YoY comparisons for non-USD markets.

2. IAIS — Global Insurance Market Report 2024

  • URL: https://www.iaisweb.org/activities-topics/financial-stability/global-insurance-market-report/
  • Key data: Global insurance premiums, insurer solvency ratios, market concentration metrics
  • Reliability: 🟢 High — IAIS is the international standard-setting body for insurance supervision
  • Notes: Published annually. More focused on regulatory/solvency metrics than market sizing. Premium figures generally align with Swiss Re within 3-5%.

3. Insurance Europe — European Insurance in Figures 2024

  • URL: https://www.insuranceeurope.eu/statistics
  • Key data: European insurance premiums by country and line of business; ~EUR 1.4T total
  • Reliability: 🟢 High — Industry body for European insurers
  • Notes: Covers 37 markets (EU + EEA + UK + Switzerland). Good breakdown by life/non-life/health. Does not publish transaction count data.

4. NAIC — US Insurance Industry at a Glance 2024

  • URL: https://content.naic.org/cipr-topics/insurance-industry-glance
  • Key data: US insurance premiums by line; P&C ~$840B, life ~$670B, health ~$1.1T
  • Reliability: 🟢 High — NAIC is the US state regulator association; data is from statutory filings
  • Notes: US-only. Excellent granularity by state and line of business. Can derive US policy counts from supplementary data.

5. Lloyd's of London — Annual Report 2024

  • URL: https://www.lloyds.com/about-lloyds/investor-relations/financial-performance/annual-reports
  • Key data: Lloyd's market GWP ~$60B; specialty and commercial lines focus
  • Reliability: 🟢 High — Audited financial data
  • Notes: Primarily commercial/specialty insurance. Useful for commercial segment sizing but not representative of global consumer insurance.

6. IRDAI — Annual Report 2023-24 (India)

  • URL: https://irdai.gov.in/annual-report
  • Key data: India insurance premiums ~$130B (INR 11T); life dominates; 650M+ policies
  • Reliability: 🟡 Medium — Government regulator data; comprehensive but reporting lags 6-12 months
  • Notes: India is the world's fastest-growing major insurance market. IRDAI provides excellent breakdowns by channel (agent, online, bank), which is valuable for payment channel analysis. LIC (state-owned) alone has 290M+ policies in force.

7. McKinsey — Global Insurance Report 2025

  • URL: https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/financial-services/our-insights/global-insurance-report
  • Key data: Insurance revenue pools, profit margins, digital adoption metrics
  • Reliability: 🟡 Medium — Consultancy estimate based on proprietary methodology
  • Notes: McKinsey's insurance reports focus on profitability and strategic themes rather than raw volume data. Useful for trend analysis and payment digitization narratives.

Secondary Sources

8. Statista — Insurance Premiums Worldwide

  • URL: https://www.statista.com/topics/1466/insurance/
  • Key data: Various insurance market size charts and projections
  • Reliability: 🟡 Medium — Aggregator; sources vary in quality
  • Notes: Useful for quick cross-checks. Primary data usually sourced from Swiss Re or AXCO.

9. Allied Market Research — Insurance Market Size 2024-2032

  • URL: https://www.alliedmarketresearch.com/insurance-market
  • Key data: Projects insurance market at $10T+ by 2032
  • Reliability: 🔴 Low — Market research firm; methodology not fully disclosed
  • Notes: Growth projections appear optimistic relative to Swiss Re forecasts. Use for directional indication only.

10. OICA — Global Vehicle Statistics

  • URL: https://www.oica.net/category/vehicles-in-use/
  • Key data: ~1.4B registered vehicles globally (2023)
  • Reliability: 🟡 Medium — Industry association; data lags by 1-2 years
  • Notes: Used as basis for auto insurance policy count estimate (~75% insurance penetration × 1.4B = ~1.05B, plus fleet and multi-vehicle → ~1.5B policies).
