Fixed Income — Source Notes

Primary Sources

SIFMA (Securities Industry and Financial Markets Association)

  • Authority: Industry association; publishes US fixed income statistics
  • Coverage: US Treasury, corporate bonds, municipal bonds, agency/MBS
  • Metrics: ADV (value), issuance, outstanding
  • Limitation: Reports VALUE not trade counts; US-focused
  • URL: https://www.sifma.org/research/statistics/

FINRA TRACE

  • Authority: Regulatory; mandatory reporting for US bond trades
  • Coverage: US corporate bonds, agency debentures, asset-backed securities
  • Metrics: Trade count, par value, prices
  • Limitation: Does NOT cover US Treasuries (separate reporting); US only
  • URL: https://www.finra.org/finra-data/fixed-income

Tradeweb

  • Authority: Major electronic fixed income trading platform
  • Coverage: Government bonds, corporate bonds, derivatives (global)
  • Metrics: Monthly total volume, ADV
  • Limitation: Covers only electronic platform volume (growing but not all trading)
  • URL: https://www.tradeweb.com/newsroom/media-center/news-releases/

ICMA European Repo Market Survey

  • Authority: Industry association; only comprehensive European repo data
  • Coverage: European repo market (EU + UK)
  • Metrics: Outstanding amounts, daily turnover, instrument mix
  • Limitation: Point-in-time survey (June/December), European only
  • URL: https://www.icmagroup.org/market-practice-and-regulatory-policy/repo-and-collateral-markets/market-data/

Secondary Sources

SIX Group

  • Global bond market sizing ($141-145T)
  • Infographic with country and currency breakdowns

Bloomberg

  • Qualitative analysis of fixed income market trends
  • Electronic trading adoption metrics

Data Gaps

  • No global centralized source for bond trade COUNTS
  • Non-US bond trade counts are poorly reported
  • Repo transaction counts are estimated — no centralized repo trade reporting exists
  • Asian and EM bond market trading data is fragmented
