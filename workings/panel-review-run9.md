## 1. Consumer Cards (24,501 TPS, conf: 91)

### 1a. Is Nilson's 772.7B comprehensive? What about prepaid and commercial?

**Verdict: Yes, Nilson's 772.73B is comprehensive for global network cards.**

Nilson Report explicitly states the 772.73B figure covers "purchase transactions
worldwide for goods and services generated by credit, debit and prepaid cards"
operating on the six global networks (Visa, UnionPay, Mastercard, Amex, JCB,
Discover/Diners Club). Prepaid cards are folded into debit totals in Nilson's
presentation. Commercial card products (corporate T&E, fleet, purchasing, small
business credit/debit/prepaid) are included in the headline figure.

**What Nilson does NOT capture:**

- **Domestic-only networks:** RuPay (India, ~12B), Mir (Russia, ~15B), Elo
  (Brazil, ~5B), BC Card (South Korea, ~4B), and others (~10B). These add
  ~46B transactions, bringing the adjusted global card total to ~819B. The
  existing report already documents this and properly excludes them from the
  primary Nilson figure.
- **Private-label store cards:** Transactions that never touch a global network
  (e.g., Nordstrom card processed on store's own system). Volume unknown but
  likely declining as most private-label cards now co-brand with Visa/MC.

**Assessment:** No revision needed. The 772.73B figure is the right anchor for
global network card transactions. The ~819B adjusted figure (with domestic
networks) is correctly documented as supplementary.

### 1b. How much of "digital wallets" is card-on-file?

**Key finding from Mastercard and Visa 2024 filings:**

- **Mastercard: 30% of all transactions in 2024 were tokenized.** That implies
  ~55.5B tokenized Mastercard transactions (30% of 185B). These include both
  card-on-file e-commerce and NFC tap-to-pay via Apple Pay/Google Pay/Samsung Pay.
- **Visa: 50% of digital transactions now use tokenization**, with 12.6B tokens
  issued by early 2025. Visa's "digital transactions" subset is smaller than total
  transactions; at ~40% digital share of 303B branded = ~121B, of which ~61B are
  tokenized.
- **Combined estimate:** ~115-120B card-network transactions in 2024 were
  tokenized (Apple Pay NFC + Google Pay NFC + card-on-file e-commerce + in-app).
  Of these, ~32B are Apple Pay/Google Pay/Samsung Pay NFC transactions that
  appear in both our Consumer Cards and Digital Wallets capsules.

**Overlap with Digital Wallets capsule:**

The existing overlap analysis estimates ~40B card-rail wallet transactions
(Apple Pay 23B + Google Pay 4.5B + Samsung Pay 2.9B + PayPal 3B + other 6.6B).
This is consistent with the tokenization data. However, "card-on-file" e-commerce
(~80B tokenized transactions that are NOT wallet-initiated) is properly counted
only in Consumer Cards, not in Digital Wallets.

**Assessment:** The overlap quantification in OVERLAP_ANALYSIS.md (~40B
card-rail wallet overlap) is well-calibrated. The card-on-file e-commerce
tokenized volume (~80B) creates no double-counting because it is counted only
in Consumer Cards (and partially in E-Commerce as an overlay). No revision needed.

### 1c. Latest Nilson / Visa / Mastercard cross-validation

**Cross-validation of the 772.73B headline:**

| Source | Figure | Reconciliation |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Nilson Report 2024 | 772.73B purchase txns | Primary anchor |
| Sum of networks (our model) | 769.6B | 0.4% gap — excellent |
| Visa 10-K FY2024: 303B branded | 303B | Consistent |
| Mastercard 10-K FY2024: 185B total | 185B | Consistent |
| Nilson projection (2022): 891.2B by 2027 | — | 2024 actual (773B) slightly ahead of Nilson's 2022 projection curve |
| Payments Dive/Nilson: 43% growth by 2029 from 2024 | ~1,105B by 2029 | Implies ~7.4% CAGR 2024-2029, consistent with our baseline |

**Assessment:** The 772.73B figure is among the best-sourced numbers in the
entire Universe of Finance. Three independent cross-checks converge within 0.4%.
Confidence score of 91 is warranted. No revision recommended.

### Panel Verdict: Consumer Cards

| Dimension | Finding | Action |

## 2. Digital Wallets (19,660 TPS, conf: 67)

### 2a. UPI classification — is overlap handled right?

**The core question:** UPI's 172B transactions appear in both Digital Wallets
(as mobile wallet transactions via PhonePe/Google Pay/Paytm) and Bank Transfers
(as A2A real-time payments). Is this handled correctly?

**Current approach in OVERLAP_ANALYSIS.md:**
- UPI is counted primarily in Bank Transfers (as the A2A rail)
- Digital Wallets subtracts UPI to yield ~448B incremental wallet transactions
- De-duplicated wallet TPS is ~12,930 (not 19,660)

**Assessment:** The overlap handling is architecturally correct. UPI is
fundamentally an interbank transfer system — PhonePe and Google Pay are UI
layers, not settlement systems. The NPCI (which operates UPI) classifies it
as a "real-time payment system," not a wallet.

**However, a presentation risk remains:** The headline Digital Wallets figure
(620B / 19,660 TPS) appears in the category report WITHOUT the overlap
adjustment. A reader scanning just the Digital Wallets report would overcount.

**Recommendation:** Add a prominent "De-duplicated TPS" row to the Digital
Wallets summary table showing the ~12,930 figure after UPI subtraction. This
is already in OVERLAP_ANALYSIS.md but should be surfaced in the category report
itself.

### 2b. Alipay/WeChat Pay opacity

**Latest data points (2024):**

- **PBOC aggregate:** ~280B mobile payment transactions in China (Statista
  corroboration; consistent with our existing estimate).
- **Non-bank payment institutions:** Account for 68% of electronic payment
  volume and 9% by value in 2024. Alipay + WeChat Pay = >90% of this segment.
- **Alipay/WeChat split:** Q3 2023 data (most recent granular data) shows
  Alipay at RMB 118.19T and WeChat Pay at RMB 67.81T, giving a ~63/37 split
  by value. The split by transaction count is unknown but likely more even
  (WeChat processes many more micro-transactions).
- **PBOC Payment System Report Q2 2024:** Available at
  camlmac.pbc.gov.cn — confirms aggregate mobile payment figures but does not
  break out individual platforms.

**Key concern:** The "1 billion daily WeChat transactions" figure cited in
BusinessOfApps (implying ~365B annual) contradicts PBOC's 280B total for ALL
mobile payments. This confirms our existing note that the "1B daily" figure
likely includes mini-program interactions, not just payments.

**Assessment:** The 280B China mobile figure is the best available anchor.
The Alipay/WeChat split is unknowable from public data but the aggregate is
well-triangulated. No revision to the 280B figure is warranted.

**Confidence impact:** The opacity of Alipay/WeChat individual volumes is the
primary reason the Digital Wallets confidence is 67, not 80+. This is an
inherent data limitation, not a methodological flaw.

### 2c. PBOC latest mobile payment data

**Confirmed 2024 figures:**
- ~280B mobile payment transactions (consistent with our estimate)
- ~969M mobile payment users as of June 2024
- 88% cashless penetration rate
- QR codes at 92.7% penetration

**New data point:** PBOC Q2 2024 Payment System Report is available and
confirms the aggregate figures. No revision needed to our 280B China estimate.

### Panel Verdict: Digital Wallets

| Dimension | Finding | Action |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| UPI classification | Correctly handled in overlap analysis | Surface de-duplicated TPS in category report |
| Alipay/WeChat opacity | 280B aggregate confirmed; individual split unknowable | None; inherent limitation |
| PBOC latest data | Consistent with existing estimates | None |
| Confidence score | 67 is appropriate given China opacity + Apple Pay uncertainty | None |
| **Overall** | **Methodology is sound. Minor presentation improvement recommended.** | **Add de-dup row to REPORT.md** |

## 3. Bank Transfers (15,338 TPS, conf: 78)

### 3a. UPI double-counting between wallets and transfers

**This is the same issue as 2a above, viewed from the other side.**

**Current approach:** Bank Transfers counts UPI as part of its 484B total
(within the 378B real-time payments subtotal). Digital Wallets is adjusted
down by 172B in the overlap matrix.

**Cross-validation of UPI figure:**
- NPCI reports 17,220 crore (172.2B) transactions in calendar 2024
- The bank-transfers report cites 131.1B (which appears to be a different
  time period — likely fiscal year FY2024 ending March 2024, vs. calendar 2024)
- **Discrepancy found:** The bank-transfers REPORT.md cites UPI at 131.1B
  but the data.json regional breakdown shows 172.2B. The REPORT.md text appears
  to use the FY2024 figure while data.json uses calendar 2024.

**Recommendation:** Standardize on calendar 2024 figure (172.2B) throughout
the bank-transfers capsule. The REPORT.md text citing "131.1B" should be
updated to 172.2B, which is the calendar-year total that aligns with the
reporting period used by all other categories.

### 3b. Non-CPMI country coverage gap

**The issue:** BIS CPMI Red Book covers ~27 member jurisdictions. The "Other"
bucket in our real-time payments breakdown is 60.1B (15.9% of RTP total),
attributed to non-CPMI countries.

**New data from BIS March 2025 commentary:**
- BIS confirms continued growth in credit transfers and e-money, especially in
  EMDEs (emerging market and developing economies)
- Fast payments are "gaining further ground" in CPMI countries
- The BIS Data Portal now includes additional countries beyond CPMI-27

**Coverage gap estimate:**
The 60.1B "Other" RTP figure is derived by subtracting known systems from
ACI's 378B global RTP total. This is the weakest part of the bank-transfers
estimate. The unattributed systems likely include:
- Indonesia (QRIS/BI-FAST): ~8-12B est.
- Philippines (InstaPay/PESONet): ~2-3B est.
- Malaysia (DuitNow): ~2-3B est.
- Vietnam (Napas 247): ~1-2B est.
- Mexico (SPEI/CoDi): ~3-5B est.
- Saudi Arabia (sarie): ~1-2B est.
- Other: ~35-45B remaining

This ~60B gap is the primary weakness keeping confidence at 78 rather than 85+.

**Recommendation:** Flag this as a research target for future runs. Searching
individual central bank annual reports (OJK Indonesia, BSP Philippines, BNM
Malaysia, SBV Vietnam, Banxico Mexico) could close 30-40% of the gap.

### 3c. SWIFT traffic data

**Latest SWIFT statistics (2024):**
- 13.4B FIN messages sent in 2024 (fastest pace in 15 years)
- Average 53.3M messages per day
- 3.4B API calls on the Swift platform
- 90% of payments reach recipient bank within one hour
- 11,500+ institutions across 224 territories

**Assessment:** SWIFT is correctly treated as a messaging layer, not a
settlement rail, and is excluded from the bank-transfer transaction count.
The 53.3M messages/day (~19.5B/year) represents coordination messages, not
settlement events. This is properly documented.

### Panel Verdict: Bank Transfers

| Dimension | Finding | Action |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| UPI double-counting | Correctly handled; Bank Transfers is primary category | Fix FY vs CY discrepancy in REPORT.md |
| Non-CPMI coverage gap | ~60B unattributed RTP is the main weakness | Flag for future research |
| SWIFT treatment | Correctly excluded as messaging layer | None |
| Confidence score | 78 is appropriate; could reach 85+ with SE Asian data | None now |
| **Overall** | **Methodology is sound. One data inconsistency to fix.** | **Standardize UPI to CY2024 (172.2B)** |

## Cross-Category Panel Summary

### De-Duplication Health Check

The three largest categories sum to:
- Raw: 773B + 620B + 484B = 1,877B
- After de-duplication (per OVERLAP_ANALYSIS.md): 773B + 408B + 484B = 1,665B
- Overlap removed: 212B (172B UPI + 40B card-rail wallets)

The de-duplication methodology is sound. The two largest overlaps (UPI and
card-rail wallets) are well-quantified and consistently applied.

### Remaining Risks

1. **China's true digital payment count may be higher than 280B.** The NBS
   retail sales cross-check suggests ~550B retail payment transactions, with
   the ~270B gap likely split between card-tap and bank-app transfers. However,
   using the PBOC mobile-specific figure (280B) for the wallet category is the
   most conservative and defensible choice.

2. **UnionPay's 247B may include wallet-initiated transactions.** Many
   Alipay/WeChat transactions charge a linked UnionPay card, creating circular
   overlap between the Consumer Cards and Digital Wallets figures. The magnitude
   is unknown but likely 20-50B. This is flagged in the reports but not
   subtracted, which is the right call given the uncertainty.

3. **Apple Pay volume uncertainty (+/-20%).** The ~24B estimate could be 20-28B.
   At the high end, card-rail overlap increases from ~40B to ~44B — not material
   to the de-duplicated total.
