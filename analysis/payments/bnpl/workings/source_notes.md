# Buy Now Pay Later (BNPL) — Source Notes

> Extended notes on each source consulted during research. Accessed 2026-03-28.

---

## Primary Sources

### 1. Klarna — H1 2024 Results / Investor Presentation
- **URL:** https://www.klarna.com/international/press/klarna-reports-strong-first-half-of-2024/
- **Key data:** ~2.5M transactions/day; 150M active consumers; ~$100B annualized GMV; 45 markets; approaching profitability
- **Reliability:** 🟢 High — Pre-IPO company with audited financials (filed S-1 in November 2024)
- **Notes:** Klarna is the largest BNPL provider by transaction count. Their "2.5M transactions/day" figure appears to be at the purchase level. Revenue mix: ~65% merchant commissions, ~35% consumer interest/fees.

### 2. Block Inc. — 10-K FY2024 (Afterpay segment)
- **URL:** SEC EDGAR filing
- **Key data:** Afterpay ~$25B GMV; 22M+ active consumers; primarily US, UK, Australia
- **Reliability:** 🟢 High — SEC-filed audited data
- **Notes:** Block acquired Afterpay for $29B in January 2022. Afterpay segment data is reported separately. Growth has been modest post-acquisition as Block focused on integration. Average order value ~$63 (lower than peers, reflecting fashion/beauty skew).

### 3. Affirm Holdings — 10-K FY2024
- **URL:** SEC EDGAR filing
- **Key data:** $28.3B GMV; 19.4M active consumers; 303K active merchants; ~$140 avg order
- **Reliability:** 🟢 High — SEC-filed audited data
- **Notes:** Affirm's fiscal year ends in June. Highest AOV of major BNPL providers, reflecting focus on high-ticket categories (travel, electronics, home improvement). Partnership with Amazon is a key growth driver. Offers 0% APR (merchant-funded) and interest-bearing loans up to 60 months.

### 4. PayPal Holdings — 10-K FY2024 (Pay Later)
- **URL:** SEC EDGAR filing
- **Key data:** $33B BNPL originations in H1 2024; Pay in 4, Pay Monthly products; 10+ countries
- **Reliability:** 🟡 Medium — PayPal discloses BNPL data at varying levels of detail across quarters
- **Notes:** PayPal's BNPL offering benefits from 430M+ active accounts and existing merchant integrations. BNPL is growing as a share of PayPal's total TPV ($1.7T). Exact annual figures require annualization from half-year disclosures.

### 5. Zip Co — FY2024 Annual Report
- **URL:** https://zip.co/investors
- **Key data:** A$11.2B TTV (~$7.5B USD); 5.6M active customers; US + Australia focus
- **Reliability:** 🟢 High — ASX-listed, audited
- **Notes:** Zip exited several international markets (UK, India, South Africa, etc.) in 2022-2023 to focus on profitability. Now primarily US + ANZ. The turnaround to profitability reduces the data sample but improves data quality.

### 6. Worldpay — Global Payments Report 2024 (BNPL section)
- **URL:** https://worldpay.globalpaymentsreport.com/
- **Key data:** BNPL at 5% of global e-commerce payments by value (2024); projected to reach 6% by 2027
- **Reliability:** 🟡 Medium — Annual survey/model by FIS/Worldpay; methodology has limitations but is widely cited
- **Notes:** Worldpay GPR is the most widely cited source for payment method market share by country. BNPL section provides country-level adoption rates. Key insight: BNPL share highest in Sweden (23%), Germany (19%), Norway (18%), Australia (13%).

### 7. Juniper Research — BNPL Market Forecasts 2024-2029
- **URL:** https://www.juniperresearch.com/research/fintech-payments/buy-now-pay-later/
- **Key data:** Projects $576B global BNPL spend by 2026; 900M+ BNPL users by 2027
- **Reliability:** 🟡 Medium — Paid research report; methodology partially disclosed
- **Notes:** Juniper's projections are generally in line with our baseline model. Their 900M user figure for 2027 is useful for cross-checking transaction-per-user ratios.

## Regulatory Sources

### 8. CFPB — Buy Now, Pay Later: Market Trends and Consumer Impacts (2024)
- **URL:** https://www.consumerfinance.gov/data-research/research-reports/
- **Key data:** BNPL users more likely to have financial distress indicators; average of 3.4 BNPL loans simultaneously; late fees growing; 20% return rates
- **Reliability:** 🟢 High — US federal regulator study
- **Notes:** CFPB has classified BNPL products as "credit cards" for regulatory purposes. This means BNPL providers must provide dispute resolution, billing statements, and refund rights equivalent to card issuers. Compliance costs could reduce margins and slow growth.

### 9. EU Consumer Credit Directive 2023/2225
- **URL:** https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32023L2225
- **Key data:** BNPL included in scope of consumer credit regulation from 2026; requires creditworthiness assessment before extending BNPL credit
- **Reliability:** 🟢 High — Published EU law
- **Notes:** This is the most significant regulatory development for European BNPL. Klarna (headquartered in Sweden) will be directly affected. Implementation deadline is November 2026 for most member states.

## Industry Analysis Sources

### 10. Bain & Company — The Future of BNPL (2024)
- **URL:** https://www.bain.com/insights/topics/payments/
- **Key data:** BNPL adoption rates by demographic; profitability analysis; competitive dynamics with card network installment products
- **Reliability:** 🟡 Medium — Consultancy analysis; well-researched but not primary data
- **Notes:** Key insight: Visa Installments and Mastercard Installments are the biggest long-term competitive threat to dedicated BNPL providers. These products run on existing card rails and don't require new merchant integrations.
