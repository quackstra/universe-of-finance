Transaction Definition

  • One contract = one futures or options contract traded on an exchange
  • Physical OTC trades: one purchase/sale agreement between counterparties
  • Does NOT include commodity swaps (covered in OTC derivatives)
  • Carbon credits: one contract = one futures/options contract (not one tCO2)

Scope

  • In scope: Exchange-traded commodity futures and options (energy, metals, agriculture, carbon/environmental), physical OTC commodity trades
  • Out of scope: Commodity swaps (OTC derivatives category), commodity ETFs (equity category), commodity-linked structured products

Double-Counting with ETD

  • ALL commodity futures and options are included in the ETD category total (205B contracts)
  • This report isolates the commodity subset (~3% = ~6.5B)
  • When aggregating Universe of Finance totals, subtract commodity from ETD to avoid double-counting

Volume Estimation

  • Primary method: FIA ~3% commodity share of global ETD
  • Cross-checked with bottom-up exchange data (ICE 1.2B + CME + LME + Chinese exchanges)
  • Chinese exchange volumes may include significant day-trading (inflated counts)

Market Hours

  • Using same methodology as ETD capsule: 252 trading days, 21.6h weighted effective hours
  • Commodities trade across US, European, and Asian sessions with varying hours

Notional Value

  • Not directly comparable across commodity types
  • Crude oil contracts ($70K each) vs. carbon contracts ($80 each) = 875x difference in value per contract
  • Total notional is an order-of-magnitude estimate only

Projection Assumptions

  • Baseline (6%): Steady growth from energy transition, climate hedging
  • High growth (10%): Carbon markets scale globally, metals super-cycle
  • Conservative (3%): Price stabilization, speculation regulation
