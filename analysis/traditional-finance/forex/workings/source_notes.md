# Forex — Source Notes

## Primary Sources

### BIS Triennial Central Bank Survey
- **Authority**: Gold standard for FX market data
- **Methodology**: Survey of ~1,200 dealers across 52 jurisdictions, conducted every 3 years in April
- **Coverage**: OTC FX spot, forwards, swaps, options (not exchange-traded FX futures)
- **Metric**: Daily turnover on a "net-net" basis (adjusted for local and cross-border double-counting)
- **Limitation**: Snapshot of April activity only; no continuous time series
- **2022 URL**: https://www.bis.org/statistics/rpfx22_fx.htm
- **2025 URL**: https://www.bis.org/statistics/rpfx25_fx.htm

### CLS Group
- **Authority**: Operates the primary multi-currency cash settlement system for FX
- **Coverage**: 18 major currencies, primarily institutional/interbank
- **Metric**: Daily settlement values and trade counts
- **Limitation**: Only covers CLS-settled trades (~55-60% of global FX by value, ~15-20% by count)
- **Monthly reports**: https://www.cls-group.com/news/fx-trade-volume-report/

## Secondary Sources

### NY Fed Foreign Exchange Committee Volume Survey
- Semi-annual survey of North American FX dealers
- Useful for US market share and instrument mix
- URL: https://www.newyorkfed.org/fxc/fx-volume-survey

### Bank of England FX Joint Standing Committee
- Quarterly survey of UK FX market
- London is the world's largest FX trading center (~38% of global volume)

## Data Gaps
- No authoritative global source for FX transaction COUNTS
- Retail FX volume is poorly measured (broker-reported, not centrally cleared)
- Non-CLS currency pairs (e.g., many EM crosses) have limited transaction data
- BIS survey is triennial — inter-survey estimates are interpolations
